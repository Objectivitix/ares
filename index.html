Annihilation begins

From the studio behind the universally acclaimed Aion trilogy comes another genre-redefining massively multiplayer experience.

Set 12 years before the events of Aion, Ares explores the origin of the Oblivion War, recounting the events that led to the apocalyptic event. Ares simulates international politics by dividing millions of players into 30 fictitious Nations, each gouverned by a unique administration with its own ideology.

You control everything

Peace or war, life or death - it's all up to the players. Ares' extremely complex System evolves in real time as players form alliances and hostilities. An intricate web of related and conflicting Objectives will set the Game in motion; Nations shall compete to be the first to complete them all. As the Game progresses, special events (no spoilers!) may trigger in real time based on the state of the System.

You play as anyone: a civilian (spectator), a soldier, a secret agent, a millionaire, a gouvernment official. If you were a significant contributor in the Aion trilogy, you can play as a higher-level politician. As you complete Missions and gain experience points, you advance in the hierarchy. But beware: go high enough and you may find yourself facing enemies coming not only from other Nations.

What's stopping everyone from just doing nothing and keeping peace?

Humans when met with material gain. The catalyst: $50 000 000 (50 million), distributed to players of the first Nation having completed all Objectives.

If less than half a million people (including NPCs) remain, the Nation with the most Objectives completed wins. If the global population remains above 100 million exactly 900 days after launch (Peace Ending), only $3 000 000 is distributed, and to all Nations.

Distribution by hierarchy: the higher your Level, the more money you win. However, if Peace Ending is reached, you'd need to be in a quite a high position to win a considerable amount.
